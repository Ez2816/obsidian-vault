# BMEG 230 – Biomechanics I

## Purpose of the course

BMEG 230 uses mechanics to describe motion, loading, deformation, musculoskeletal forces, and device–tissue interactions. This page is a conceptual map, not a reconstruction of the official syllabus.

## Main conceptual units

Kinematics vs Kinetics; Free-Body Diagrams in Biomechanics; Force and Moment Equilibrium; Two-Dimensional Rigid-Body Dynamics; Moment Arms and Muscle Forces; Stress, Strain, and Material Behaviour; Axial, Shear, and Bending Loading; Load Sharing Between Bone and Fixation Plates; Plate Thickness and Bending Stiffness; Mechanical Stability and Bone Healing.
