# BMEG 257 – Biomedical Engineering Design I

## Purpose of the course

BMEG 257 organizes biomedical design around stakeholder needs, requirements, concepts, prototypes, risk, verification, validation, and documentation. This page is a conceptual map, not a reconstruction of the official syllabus.

## Main conceptual units

Engineering Design Process; Needs Finding and User Interviews; User Needs vs Design Requirements; Requirements Traceability; Functional Decomposition; Concept Generation; Weighted Decision Matrices; Computer-Aided Design; Rapid Prototyping; Proof-of-Concept Prototyping; Verification vs Validation; Engineering Risk Management; Design History Files; Ethics and Sustainability in Biomedical Design; Inertial Measurement Units.
