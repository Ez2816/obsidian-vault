# BMEG 250 – Cellular Physiology and Biophysics

## Purpose of the course

BMEG 250 explains cellular transport, electrical behaviour, signalling, mechanochemistry, and measurement using physical principles. This page is a conceptual map, not a reconstruction of the official syllabus.

## Main conceptual units

Surface Area vs Volume in Cell Biology; Estimating Lipids in a Cell Membrane; Membrane Transport; Electrochemical Gradients and Membrane Potential; Cell Signalling and Signal Transduction; Muscle Mechanochemistry; Neurotransmission; Light vs Electron Microscopy; Hierarchical Organization from Cells to Tissues.

## Concepts I previously clarified

- [[Surface Area vs Volume in Cell Biology]]: Earlier discussion asked why membrane calculations use surface area rather than cell volume: membrane-associated quantities scale with area, while intracellular quantities scale with volume.
- [[Estimating Lipids in a Cell Membrane]]: Earlier discussion highlighted when the bilayer requires a factor of two: each leaflet occupies approximately the full projected membrane area.
