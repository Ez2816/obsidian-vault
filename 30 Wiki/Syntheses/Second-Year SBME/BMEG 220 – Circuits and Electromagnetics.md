# BMEG 220 – Circuits and Electromagnetics with Application to Biomedical Engineering

## Purpose of the course

BMEG 220 connects circuit models and electromagnetic fields to biomedical signals, membranes, electrodes, and instrumentation. This page is a conceptual map, not a reconstruction of the official syllabus.

## Main conceptual units

Charge, Current, Voltage, and Resistance; Kirchhoff's Laws; Circuit Analysis Methods; Capacitors and Inductors; Transient vs Steady-State Circuit Response; Impedance and Reactance; Phase in AC Circuits; Phasors vs Instantaneous Signals; RC, RL, and RLC Circuits; Electrical Resonance; Passive Filters and Frequency Response; Operational Amplifier Fundamentals; Electric Fields and Electric Potential; Capacitance and Dielectrics; Magnetic Fields and Current; Magnetic Flux and Faraday's Law; Bioeffects of Electromagnetic Fields; Physiological Electrical Equivalent Circuits.
