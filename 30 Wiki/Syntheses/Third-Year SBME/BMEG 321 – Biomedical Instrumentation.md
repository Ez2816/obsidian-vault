# BMEG 321 – Biomedical Instrumentation

## Official scope

BMEG 321 is a 3-credit course on the working principles of medical instruments used to measure nervous, respiratory, and circulatory systems, with particular attention to biopotential signals. It also introduces medical imaging and instrumentation used in minimally invasive and robotic surgery.

## Main course content

- Biomedical measurement chains and instrumentation architecture
- Biopotential generation, electrodes, and acquisition
- Nervous-system measurements
- Respiratory measurements and sensors
- Circulatory and cardiac measurements
- Signal conditioning, amplification, filtering, and noise
- Instrument calibration, safety, and measurement limitations
- Introductory medical-imaging instrumentation
- Instruments for minimally invasive surgery
- Instrumentation in robotic surgery

## Prerequisite context

The listed prerequisites require differential-equation preparation and prior circuits or electromagnetics coursework. The course is credit-excluded with ELEC 371.

## Awaiting course materials

Exact instruments, circuit designs, laboratory experiments, safety standards, signal-processing methods, and imaging modalities remain to be verified.
