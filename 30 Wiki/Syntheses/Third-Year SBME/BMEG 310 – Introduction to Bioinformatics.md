# BMEG 310 – Introduction to Bioinformatics

## Official scope

BMEG 310 is a 4-credit course on the principles behind bioinformatics methods and the use of databases and tools to solve problems in genomics, proteomics, structural biology, and evolutionary biology.

## Main course content

- Biological sequence data and common data representations
- Bioinformatics databases and data retrieval
- Sequence comparison and alignment principles
- Genomic data and genome-level questions
- Proteomic data and protein-focused analysis
- Structural-biology information and computational tools
- Evolutionary relationships and phylogenetic reasoning
- Selecting and interpreting bioinformatics tools
- Computational workflows, outputs, and limitations

## Prerequisite context

The official prerequisites are BMEG 245 and one of CPEN 221 or CPEN 223.
