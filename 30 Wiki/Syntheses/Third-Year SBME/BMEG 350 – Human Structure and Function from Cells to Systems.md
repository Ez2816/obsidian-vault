# BMEG 350 – Human Structure and Function from Cells to Systems

## Official scope

BMEG 350 is a 4-credit course in human anatomy, histology, and physiology using biomedical-engineering examples. Its published scope includes anatomical compartmentation, regulation of function, homeostatic control, intercellular communication, and assessment of cellular and organ function.

## Main course content

- Human anatomical organization and terminology
- Histological organization of tissues
- Relationships between structure and physiological function
- Anatomical compartments and transport between them
- Regulation of organ and system function
- Homeostasis and controlled physiological variables
- Sensors, control centres, and effectors in homeostatic systems
- Intercellular communication strategies
- Cellular-function assessment
- Organ-function assessment

## Prerequisite context

The official prerequisites are BMEG 245 and BMEG 250.
