# BMEG 371 – Transport Phenomena within Cells and Tissues

## Official scope

BMEG 371 is a 3-credit mathematical treatment of fluid flow and mass transport in healthy and diseased cells and tissues, including drug-delivery and tissue-engineering applications.

## Main course content

- Conservation principles for mass and momentum
- Control volumes and continuum descriptions
- Fluid-flow variables and constitutive assumptions
- Diffusion and concentration gradients
- Convection and combined convective-diffusive transport
- Transport across membranes and tissue boundaries
- Characteristic scales and dimensionless reasoning
- Initial and boundary conditions
- Healthy versus diseased transport environments
- Drug-delivery transport problems
- Tissue-engineering transport constraints

## Prerequisite context

The official prerequisites require multivariable calculus and differential-equation preparation. BMEG 371 is listed as equivalent to CHBE 470.

## Awaiting course materials

Exact governing equations, coordinate systems, approximations, dimensionless numbers, numerical methods, and application problems remain to be verified.
