# Audience, Purpose, and Technical Context

## Plain-language overview

Audience, Purpose, and Technical Context is a foundational idea in BMEG 201. In plain language, it provides a disciplined way to describe, predict, evaluate, or communicate part of a biomedical engineering system. The important move is to identify what is being represented, what assumptions make the representation useful, and what evidence would show that it is inadequate.

## Academic foundation

The foundation includes identifying the audience, identifying the intended action, selecting an appropriate level of detail, technical versus non-technical audiences, assumptions about prior knowledge, preserving accuracy while simplifying. These ideas should be interpreted as a connected model rather than an isolated vocabulary list.

## Common reasoning mistakes

- Treating a model, diagram, or definition as the physical system itself.
- Applying terminology or a relationship without checking its assumptions, units, direction, scale, or intended use.
- Confusing an observed result with the interpretation used to explain it.
- Extending a simplified course example to a biological or clinical claim without supporting evidence.
